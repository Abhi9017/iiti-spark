TERMS OF USE

Respect, consent, authenticity.

IITI Spark is an independent student prototype and is not an official IIT Indore service, programme, or endorsement.

Eligibility

You must be 18 or older and use an active, verified @iiti.ac.in account. You may create only one authentic profile and may not impersonate another person.

Consent and conduct

A match does not imply consent to meet, share personal information, or engage in any activity. Harassment, threats, hate, stalking, sexual coercion, doxxing, unsolicited explicit content, and attempts to bypass blocks are prohibited.

Safety

Use report and block controls. Meet in public places, tell a trusted person, and do not share passwords, financial details, OTPs, or sensitive documents. In an emergency, contact appropriate campus or public authorities.

Moderation

The operator may review reported content, restrict accounts, preserve evidence when legally required, or remove the service. Reports should be handled confidentially and only by authorized moderators.

No guarantee

This prototype is provided as-is. Identity is limited to Google account verification; it does not independently verify every profile statement.

PRIVACY NOTICE

Know what is stored.

The prototype stores your Google account identifier, institute email, profile fields, swipes, matches, reports, blocks, session records, and messages in Google Sheets. Optional uploaded photos are stored in Google Drive.

It does not collect or store your Google password. Google sign-in returns a short-lived identity token that the backend verifies. The app should store only a hashed application-session token.

Chats are not end-to-end encrypted. Do not send confidential, medical, financial, intimate, or legally sensitive information.

Profile cards do not expose institute email addresses. Administrators with access to the backend spreadsheet can access stored information and must follow a strict least-privilege policy.

You can turn off discovery, block users, report misuse, and request account deletion. Deletion anonymizes primary profile fields; legally required safety or audit records may need separate handling by the operator.
